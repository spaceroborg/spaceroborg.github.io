Projects & Research

Shared curiosity for the natural world offers a fertile foundation for interdisciplinary innovation. My university research focused on bio-inspired aerial robotics — exploring how nature can inform the design of intelligent machines to interact meaningfully with the environment. Here you'll find an overview of my completed open-source projects, along with other contributions in the area of robotics and UAVs.

Please email me if you're interested in my full portfolio.

MEng Thesis - Optical Flow Guided Soft-Grasping Aerial Robot for Aquatic Waste Management

Designed and developed an autonomous aerial robot for collecting surface debris from water bodies. The system used optical flow divergence for altitude control and smooth landings over water.

View Project

MSc Thesis - Biomimetic Micro-glider for Autonomous Thermal Soaring

Designed and built a lightweight, portable glider inspired by the structure of dragonfly wings to explore autonomous thermal soaring mimicking the flight of soaring birds.

View Project

NASA-Boeing High-Lift Common Research Model

Developed a NASA-Boeing experimental wing into an instrumented, modular, rapid prototyping, low-cost composite structure for acquiring CFD validation data. £100k grant from Boeing UK.

View Project

PixyBot Car Race Challenge

For a competitive robotics challenge, I developed and implemented a bio-inspired visual navigation system for a differential-drive robot using a PixyCam.

High-Fidelity Haptic Rendering of Soft Objects in VR using UAVs

Summer Internship: MIT-Skoltech joint research. I contributed to DandelionTouch, a novel VR haptic feedback system using a swarm of drones to deliver tactile sensations to the user's fingertips.
